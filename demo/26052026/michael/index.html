Explains a pattern from your diagnostic methodology. Designed to attract business owners who recognise themselves in the problem.
Annual financial statements are not financial management. Most business owners see their numbers once a year. Their accountant produces a report. They review it, nod, and carry on. The problem is not the accountant. The problem is the timing. By the time the annual statement lands, the business has already made twelve months of decisions without real data. Cash flow problems that started in month four are visible in month twelve. By then, the cost of correcting them is three times what it would have been at the source. Financial clarity is not a year-end exercise. It is a weekly discipline. The businesses that grow without constant financial stress are not the ones with better accountants. They are the ones with better management reporting systems. What gets measured monthly gets managed. What gets measured annually gets regretted.

Handraiser Post
Designed to surface qualified buyers who self-identify with a specific financial pain. CTA drives CLARITY comments from your ICP.
The hardest part of running a product-based business is not the sales. It is knowing whether the cash you are expecting to receive will arrive before the cash you owe goes out. Most business owners are managing this manually. Spreadsheets updated once a week. Numbers that are already five days out of date by the time they are reviewed. The result is a business that is technically profitable and chronically short of cash. Working capital mismanagement is not a character flaw. It is a systems problem. The fix is a cash flow forecast that updates weekly, accounts for stock on the floor, and flags timing gaps before they become crises. That is a solvable problem for any business doing more than a million in revenue. Three patterns that signal a working capital problem before it surfaces in the bank account: - Stock sitting longer than the creditor payment cycle - Revenue recognised before cash clears - No weekly visibility on the gap between debtors and creditors If any of those sound familiar, this is fixable.
