Teaching Post
Explains a pattern from Dave's methodology. Designed to build authority with the Seed-to-Series-B founder audience.
Most founders think the financial model is wrong. It rarely is. When the actuals come in below forecast, there are two possible explanations. Either the assumptions were off. Or the business did not execute the plan. Most founders cannot tell which one it is. So they do one of two things. They re-forecast — when they should be fixing operations. Or they grind harder on execution — when the plan was never realistic to begin with. The model is not the problem. The gap between the model and the actuals is information. Founders who treat that gap as noise get stuck in cycles. Founders who treat it as signal start making decisions that compound over time. A financial model that never gets fed real numbers is a document. One that does is a diagnostic tool. The difference is not in how it was built. It is in how it is used.
