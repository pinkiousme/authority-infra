Teaching Post
Most B2B service companies in the petrochemical sector run business development the same way. The founder knows the plant managers. The plant managers trust the founder. Calls come in. Proposals go out. It works. Until it stops working. The ceiling is predictable. There are only so many operator relationships one person can maintain at once. When those relationships age, change roles, or retire, the pipeline ages with them. Three things separate the companies that scale through this ceiling from those that plateau at it. First, they map existing producer relationships to current procurement cycles, not historical contracts. A contact who moved from plant manager to VP of Operations two years ago is worth a different conversation today. Most service companies have not made that call. Second, they build a commercial playbook that a BD hire can execute independently, not just the founder. The sector knowledge that closes deals stays locked in one person's head because no one has ever structured it into a repeatable motion. Third, they bring in a fractional CRO who already understands the buyer, the procurement cycle, and the relationship map. Not a generalist sales leader who needs 18 months to learn what a turnaround coordinator actually controls. Someone who knows which plant managers are making scope decisions this quarter. The relationship is not the strategy. The relationship is the entry point. The strategy is what turns entry points into a predictable, structured pipeline. Comment PIPELINE and I will send a diagnostic on whether your commercial model is built to scale beyond the founder.
