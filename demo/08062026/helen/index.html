Teaching Post
Demonstrates your diagnostic thinking for PE-backed manufacturing founders · builds authority before they need you
Three things PE-backed manufacturers get wrong about fractional CFO engagements. Not the hiring part. That decision usually gets made correctly once the need is obvious. The mistakes happen before that. First: they wait for the audit to surface the problem. By the time an auditor flags the control weakness or the reporting gap, the issue has been compounding for twelve months. A fractional CFO in place before the audit finds it first. Second: they scope the role around the close cycle and stop there. Month-end close matters. But working capital management, lender reporting, and inventory accounting are where the real cash decisions live. Scoping narrow means solving narrow. Third: they hire a Controller to fill the gap and expect CFO-level output. Controllers execute. CFOs design the system the Controller works inside. Different job. The companies that get the most from fractional CFO leadership define the scope before the engagement starts, not after the problems are already visible. If you are a founder or president at a PE-backed manufacturer and one of these three resonates, worth a conversation.
