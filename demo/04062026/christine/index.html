Teaching Post
Demonstrates Christine's diagnostic methodology. Peer-level, declarative, no closing question. Written for the founder audience, not the consultant community.
Hiring more people won't fix a broken process. It will execute that process faster, at higher volume, and with more confidence than any human team would. Most founders know this. They still do it. The logic isn't irrational. More hands means more output. More output means more revenue. The math seems right until the coordination cost shows up three months later. Here is what actually happens. A team member in a role that didn't have a clear accountability structure before they arrived invents one. It works for them. It doesn't work for the person next to them. Nobody is wrong. The process just isn't designed. Add another hire. The invented structure scales. Now you have two people doing the same thing differently and a manager spending their time translating between them. The process problem didn't get solved. It got staffed. Before the next hire, ask three questions. Is the process sound? Is the accountability structure clear? Is the role equipped to handle what it actually requires? If any answer is no, you are not solving the problem. You are funding it.
