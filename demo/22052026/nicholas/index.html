Teaching Post

Most business owners think their books are fine. They filed the taxes. Everything balanced. Here's the problem: tax-ready and deal-ready are not the same document. They are not even close. A tax return tells the IRS what happened. A clean data room tells a buyer, lender, or investor what the business actually is. The gap between those two is where most deals stall, reprice, or fall apart entirely. It is almost never the business itself. It is owner compensation buried across three line items. Personal expenses running through operations. A chart of accounts that made sense in year two but not now. None of that is fraud. All of it is a problem. A buyer prices in the confusion. A lender adds a risk premium. An investor asks why the owner needs to be in every room. The business is worth more than the books say. The books just haven't been built to prove it.
