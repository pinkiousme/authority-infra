Create. Get a transcript of your long-form piece and pull one newsletter issue from it. Written both to be found (SEO structure, keywords) and to convert a reader into a reply — the same piece does both jobs.
2
Distribute. Publish it as a LinkedIn Newsletter issue, not a standalone article. A newsletter issue is technically an article with a distribution layer added: same Google indexing, plus a guaranteed push to every subscriber's inbox and notifications. Publishing both separately just doubles the work for the same reader benefit.
3
Capture. Every piece needs one specific mechanism for someone to raise their hand — a Handraiser post with a comment-to-DM mechanic, or a live scored tool they complete on their own. A soft "let me know what you think" is not a mechanism.
4
Convert. Whoever raises their hand enters a specific message sequence, not an ad-hoc reply. This is the stage most solo consultants skip entirely, and it's usually the single biggest point of loss in the whole pipeline.
03 Copy-paste templates
The Handraiser Post
Capture stage
I put together [the specific asset: score, checklist, template] that [the exact outcome it gives them]. It covers: [Specific thing 1] [Specific thing 2] [Specific thing 3] It's free. Comment "[ONE WORD]" and I'll send it over. (Must be a connection so I can DM you — feel free to connect first.)
Why it works: the comment mechanic turns passive reach into a DM conversation, which is where the Convert stage actually starts. Every commenter enters the DM script in section 04.
The Newsletter Issue Structure
Create + Distribute stage
TITLE: [Number or How-to] + [specific outcome] + [for whom] HOOK (2-3 sentences): Open with the moment of pain from your source content, the thing your audience is doing right now that's costing them. No warm-up. SECTION 1 — The real problem: [Reframe what they think the problem is vs. what it actually is, pulled from your own transcript.] SECTION 2 — The shift: [The core insight from your content, in your own phrasing.] SECTION 3 — How to apply it: [3-5 concrete steps, short lines, each starting with a verb.] CLOSER (declarative, not a question): [One sentence on what changes when they do this.] [One clear next step.]
Publish this as a Newsletter issue, not a standalone Article — one piece, both the indexing and the inbox-push benefit.
04 The comment-to-call DM script

When someone comments on your Handraiser post, this 3-message sequence delivers the asset, starts a real conversation, and earns the call, without pitch-slapping. Send message 1 immediately, message 2 only after they reply, message 3 only if their reply shows real pain.

Message 1 — Deliver, then one question
Send immediately
Hey [name] — here's the [asset] you asked for: [link] Quick question while I have you: what made you grab this one — are you [situation A] or [situation B]?
An either/or question earns far more replies than "let me know if you have questions" because it's effortless to answer.
Message 2 — Give value on their answer
Send after they reply
That makes sense. The thing I see most [people in their situation] hit is [the specific obstacle] — [one genuinely useful tip, 1-2 sentences, no pitch]. Out of curiosity, what's your current process for [the relevant task]?
You're diagnosing, not selling. Their answer tells you whether they're a real prospect.
Message 3 — The earned ask
Only if their reply shows the pain
Got it — that's exactly the pattern I work on with [your ICP]. Happy to walk you through how I'd approach it for your content specifically. No charge, no deck, 30 minutes, you leave with a plan either way: [your booking link]
"You leave with a plan either way" removes the risk of saying yes. If message 2 showed no real pain, don't send this one.
